My name is Alan. I've lived in China for the past 4 years as an English teacher. In one of those years I was lucky enough to get a scholarship to study Chinese Mandarin with the British Council. The ability to live in China while being able to really connect with local people and interact with Chinese speakers has greatly shaped my international perspective and understanding of the world.

Christmas day as Generation UK British Council students celebrate on the roof of the international student block Wuhan, China

At university I studied politics and was always interested in international development after charitable and business projects I completed in Kenya, 2012 and 2015. Those experiences even inspired me to invest in sustainable farming for rural farmers. I and my partner's sugar cane venture has proven to be quite successful, raising £3000 in net profit. In 2020 we aim to move into a tree farming business.

Alan with his business partner Godfrey who he met during a charity program in 2012.

I'm a logical person who loves learning and growing, and so last year I decided to commit to developing skills in tech by joining a coding bootcamp with Le Wagon, Chengdu. What I love about learning code is realising the power I have to create and bring ideas into reality. That's what I want to do now: to work for a company or organisation who wants to use tech to benefit the world. I've been honing my skills creating fun little projects in the past year. I'm also very close to completing an online learning platform for my students so they can learn online through video.
